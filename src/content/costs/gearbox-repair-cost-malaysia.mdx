Nobody budgets for a gearbox. It's the repair that turns up uninvited, and for the same symptom you can collect quotes ranging from RM 300 to RM 15,000, sometimes on the same afternoon. Having watched a lot of those quotes get written, here's the honest 2026 price landscape, so the next number someone gives you lands with some context around it.

## The price ladder

| Work | Typical cost (RM) |
| --- | --- |
| Specialist diagnosis (road test + scan) | 100 – 300 |
| Fluid & filter service | 250 – 600 |
| Solenoid replacement | 800 – 2,500 |

Two things jump out of that table once you've stared at it long enough. Most real-world gearbox problems live in the top half, whatever the market's quoting habits might suggest. And the canyon between overhaul money and new-unit money is the entire reason the specialist repair trade exists.

## What actually moves the number

**The gearbox, not the badge.** A Proton X50's wet dual-clutch costs more to rebuild than a Perodua 4AT because of what's inside the casing, not what's on the bonnet. Continental units cost more again because the parts arrive priced in euros and the diagnostic software is specialist-only.

**How early you walked in.** The same JATCO CVT is a RM 2,000 repair when the whine starts and a RM 7,000 overhaul six months later. Of everything on this page, timing is the only lever entirely in your hands.

**Parts tier.** Genuine, quality aftermarket (frequently from the same production line, minus the logo), and bazaar-grade. Prices differ by three times; service life differs by ten. A quote means nothing until you know which tier it's built on, so ask.

**What's included.** One shop's overhaul price includes the torque converter, a cooler flush and twelve months of warranty. Another's includes none of those. Cheaper isn't cheaper when the old converter goes back in dirty.

## Quick brand notes

- **Perodua and Proton 4AT:** the friendliest overhauls in the market, RM 2,500 to 3,500, parts everywhere.
- **Proton Punch CVT:** RM 3,500 to 5,000 rebuilt properly, and insist on the updated start-clutch parts.
- **Honda CVT:** a big share of judder cases resolve under RM 500. Converter work RM 2,000 to 3,500, full overhaul RM 4,500 to 7,000.
- **Toyota:** failures skew fluid-related, so repairs cluster at the cheap end. Overhauls RM 3,500 to 6,500.
- **Nissan (JATCO CVT):** the family most likely to need the full rebuild. Budget RM 4,500 to 7,500 with updated parts.
- **VW and Audi DSG:** mechatronic RM 2,500 to 5,000, clutch pack RM 3,000 to 5,500. Both together on a DQ200 approaches RM 9,000.
- **BMW and Mercedes:** a surprising share of cases end at electrical or mechatronic fixes, RM 1,500 to 5,000. Full overhauls RM 7,000 to 12,000 and up, which still undercuts the dealer's replacement unit by half or more.

## Five questions that keep a quote honest

1. What exactly failed, and what's the evidence? There should be a diagnosis behind the number, not a shrug.
2. Which parts are included, at what tier, and is the torque converter in the price?
3. Is the cooler circuit being flushed or replaced?
4. What's the warranty, in months and kilometres, in writing?
5. If the teardown finds more, how does that get quoted and who approves it?

A workshop that answers all five without getting defensive is a workshop you can leave your keys with.
