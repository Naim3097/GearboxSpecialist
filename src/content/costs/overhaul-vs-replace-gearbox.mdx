There's a moment in every serious gearbox conversation where repair stops being an option and the question becomes: **overhaul this unit, buy a recond one, or pay the manufacturer for new?** The three paths differ by five figures at the extremes, and the cheapest-looking one is often the most expensive over three years.

## The three paths, priced

| Path | Typical cost (RM) | Warranty norm | The real risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Overhaul your unit | 2,500 – 12,000 by type | 6–12 months | Quality of the builder |
| Recond / used unit | 1,500 – 8,000 | 1–3 months, often verbal | Unknown history |
| New from manufacturer | 15,000 – 40,000+ | 12–24 months | Only the price |

## Why overhaul usually wins

Your gearbox's casing, shafts and gearsets — the hard parts — rarely wear out. What fails is the soft tissue: friction plates, seals, bearings, solenoids. An overhaul replaces all of it with new, **and** fixes the model's documented weak points with updated parts, inside a casing whose history you know completely.

A recond unit is someone else's gearbox with someone else's history. The paint is new; the mileage isn't. Some are honestly rebuilt. Many are simply *used* — pulled from a scrapyard car whose own fluid history is a mystery. From outside, the two are indistinguishable, which is precisely the problem.

## When recond genuinely makes sense

- **Your hard parts are destroyed** — a cracked casing, a shattered planetary set. Now the donor's hard parts are the point.
- **The unit is obsolete** and overhaul parts are unobtainable.
- **A verified rebuilder** offers a documented unit with a real written warranty — this exists, and it's effectively buying an overhaul off the shelf.

Outside those cases, the RM 1,000–2,000 saved over an overhaul is paying for risk, not value.

## When new makes sense

Rarely — the pricing is designed for insurance claims and warranty work, not retail decisions. It's rational for nearly-new cars where resale documentation matters, or where a manufacturer goodwill contribution closes the gap.

## The three-year view

Price the paths over three years, not one invoice. An overhaul at RM 5,000 with a 12-month warranty that runs trouble-free costs RM 5,000. A recond at RM 3,500 that fails in month five — outside its 90-day warranty — costs RM 3,500 plus removal, plus the *next* unit, plus the days off the road: routinely RM 8,000+ and the worst outcome in the table.

The gearbox decision is one of the few times in car ownership when the middle option is a trap and the "expensive" option is the cheap one. Get the teardown report, read the warranty, and pay for certainty.
