Nobody budgets for a gearbox. It's the repair that arrives uninvited, quoted in numbers that range — for the same symptom — from RM 300 to RM 15,000. This guide lays out the real 2026 price landscape, so the number you're quoted has context before you say yes.

## The price ladder at a glance

| Work | Typical cost (RM) |
| --- | --- |
| Specialist diagnosis (road test + scan) | 100 – 300 |
| Fluid & filter service | 250 – 600 |
| Solenoid replacement | 800 – 2,500 |
| Valve body repair / exchange | 1,500 – 4,000 |
| Torque converter (incl. removal) | 1,800 – 3,500 |
| Mechatronic repair (DSG/ZF) | 2,500 – 5,000 |
| Full overhaul — local makes 4AT | 2,500 – 3,500 |
| Full overhaul — Japanese AT/CVT | 3,500 – 7,500 |
| Full overhaul — DSG/DCT | 5,000 – 9,000 |
| Full overhaul — continental AT | 7,000 – 12,000+ |
| New unit from manufacturer | 15,000 – 40,000+ |

Two things stand out. First, **most gearbox problems live in the top half of the table** — the four-figure fears are real but less common than the market's quoting habits suggest. Second, the gap between overhaul and new-unit pricing is the entire reason the specialist repair industry exists.

## What moves the number

**The gearbox, not the car badge.** A Proton X50's 7DCT costs more to rebuild than a Perodua 4AT because of what's inside, not what's on the bonnet. Continental units cost more because parts arrive in euros and the tooling and software are specialist-only.

**How early you came in.** The same JATCO CVT is a RM 2,000 repair when the whine starts and a RM 7,000 overhaul six months later. Timing is the biggest price lever you control.

**Parts tier.** OEM, quality aftermarket (often the same production line), and bazaar-grade parts can differ 3× in price and 10× in life. A quote is meaningless without knowing which tier it's built on.

**What's actually included.** One workshop's "overhaul" includes the torque converter, cooler flush and warranty; another's doesn't. Cheaper isn't cheaper if the converter goes back in dirty.

## Brand-by-brand quick reference

- **Perodua / Proton (4AT):** the most affordable overhauls in the market — RM 2,500–3,500 with parts everywhere.
- **Proton Punch CVT:** RM 3,500–5,000 overhauled with updated start-clutch parts.
- **Honda CVT:** judder cases often resolve for under RM 500; converter work RM 2,000–3,500; overhaul RM 4,500–7,000.
- **Toyota CVT/AT:** repairs cluster low because failures are usually fluid-related; overhauls RM 3,500–6,500.
- **Nissan (JATCO CVT):** the most overhaul-prone family — budget RM 4,500–7,500 and insist on updated parts.
- **VW/Audi DSG:** mechatronic RM 2,500–5,000; clutch pack RM 3,000–5,500; both together approach RM 9,000.
- **BMW/Mercedes:** electrical and mechatronic fixes RM 1,500–5,000 solve a large share of cases; full overhauls RM 7,000–12,000+ — still less than half of BMW's or Mercedes' unit price.

## Five questions that keep a quote honest

1. What exactly failed, and what evidence shows it? (There should be a diagnosis, not a shrug.)
2. What parts are included, at which tier — and is the torque converter in the price?
3. Is the cooler circuit being flushed or replaced?
4. What's the warranty in months **and** kilometres, in writing?
5. If the teardown finds more, how is that quoted and approved?

A workshop that answers all five without flinching is a workshop you can hand keys to.

## The rule that saves the most money

Spend the first RM 300 on diagnosis, every time. It's the only purchase in this entire guide that makes every other number smaller.
