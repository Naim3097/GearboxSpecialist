An automatic that won't shift feels catastrophic. Sometimes it is. More often it's one of a handful of well-understood faults — and the cheap causes are more common than the expensive ones. Here they are, in the order a good workshop rules them out.

## 1. Low fluid (from a leak you haven't seen)

Line pressure moves everything inside an automatic. Lose fluid and the gearbox loses its ability to apply clutches — first as lazy, flaring shifts, then as missing gears. Check for drips where you park; on most modern cars a workshop-level check is needed since dipsticks are gone.

**Cost: RM 100–300** plus fixing the leak. Always the first check.

## 2. Degraded fluid

Fluid that's black and burnt-smelling can't build stable pressure and can't cool the clutches. The gearbox shifts harshly when cold, lazily when hot, and eventually throws itself into limp mode on long drives.

**Cost: RM 250–500** for a proper service — and on marginal cases it genuinely restores shifting.

## 3. A dying battery or voltage fault

Modern gearboxes are computers. Low system voltage — an aging battery, a weak alternator, corroded grounds — throws control units into fault states, and "Transmission Malfunction" on a BMW or Mercedes is frequently a RM 500 battery, not a RM 15,000 gearbox. Any electrical work should start with a scan and voltage test.

**Cost: RM 300–800.** The most under-diagnosed cause on continental cars.

## 4. Failed shift solenoids

Solenoids are the electric valves that route pressure to each clutch. When one sticks or dies, its specific gear goes missing or bangs on engagement — often with a precise fault code pointing at it.

**Cost: RM 800–2,500** depending on access; on some units the pan drops and it's an afternoon's work.

## 5. Valve body wear

The valve body is the gearbox's hydraulic brain, and its precision bores wear — especially on high-mileage units run on old fluid. Symptoms are messy: inconsistent shift quality, harsh one day and slurred the next, multiple vague codes. Mercedes 722.9 conductor plates and ZF mechatronic sleeves live in this category too.

**Cost: RM 1,500–4,000** for rebuild or exchange — still far below overhaul.

## 6. Torque converter failure

Shudder at cruising speed, stalling when shifting into gear, or rattles at idle. The converter couples the engine to the gearbox; when its lock-up clutch or internals fail, debris flows straight into everything else, so it rarely stays a converter-only problem for long.

**Cost: RM 1,800–3,500** including removal.

## 7. Worn clutch packs — the real mechanical failure

Slipping under load, flaring between gears, burnt fluid that keeps coming back. Friction material is gone and no fluid or solenoid will bring it back; this is overhaul territory.

**Cost: RM 3,500–8,000+** depending on the unit. The number everyone fears — and the least common of the seven.

## The order matters

Notice the structure: the cheapest causes are checked in an hour, and five of the seven cost less than half an overhaul. The expensive mistake isn't the repair — it's skipping diagnosis and letting a workshop start at number 7 because "gearbox spoilt, must rebuild". Insist on the scan, the voltage test and the pressure readings first.
