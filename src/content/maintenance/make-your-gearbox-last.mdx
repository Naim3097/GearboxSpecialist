Some gearboxes reach 300,000 km on original internals. Others from the same factory die before 120,000. The difference is almost never luck and almost always three decisions the owner controls: fluid, heat, and how the first symptom was treated.

## Decision one: fluid, on schedule, to specification

Everything the gearbox does — shifting, cooling, clamping the CVT belt, lubricating — it does with fluid. The full schedule lives in our [service interval guide](/maintenance/gearbox-service-schedule-malaysia); the short version is 40,000–60,000 km intervals in Malaysian conditions, always to the exact specification, with the level set by the proper procedure.

If you take one action after reading this site, make it a fluid service with a condition report.

## Decision two: manage heat

Heat is the actual killer; wear is just how it presents.

- **Drive the first two minutes gently.** Full throttle on a cold gearbox is concentrated wear.
- **In long crawls, leave gaps and roll.** Creeping constantly at 2 km/h keeps the converter slipping at zero airflow — rolling stops-and-gaps run measurably cooler.
- **Use manual mode on long climbs** so the gearbox holds one ratio instead of hunting.
- **Fix cooling problems immediately.** A marginal radiator or blocked transmission cooler quietly cooks the gearbox for months before anything feels wrong.

## Decision three: respect the first symptom

Gearboxes escalate on a schedule: a judder becomes a slip becomes a limp becomes a rebuild. The cost roughly triples at each stage. The owners who get 300,000 km aren't the ones who never had symptoms — they're the ones who booked a diagnosis in the same week the first one appeared.

## The habits that cost nothing

- **Stop completely before D–R changes.** Rolling direction changes make clutches absorb the car's momentum.
- **Handbrake first on slopes, then P.** Parking pawls that carry the car's weight on a hill wear the mechanism and make that clunk you've learned to ignore.
- **Don't launch from N.** Revving in neutral and slotting D is a shock load with no benefit.
- **Tow within limits, and add a cooler if you tow often.**

## What deliberately doesn't matter much

Plenty of gearbox folklore burns attention without protecting anything: shifting to N at every red light, warming up by idling for ten minutes, premium fuel "for the gearbox", magnetic drain-plug miracles. A healthy gearbox doesn't need rituals. It needs clean fluid, moderate temperatures, and an owner who acts on week one of a symptom instead of month six.

## The 300,000 km formula, complete

Fluid to spec every 40,000–60,000 km. Gentle first two minutes. Manual mode on climbs. Full stops before reversing. A diagnosis within a week of any new symptom. That's the entire secret — five habits, none of them expensive, and the most expensive component in your car quietly outlasts everything around it.
